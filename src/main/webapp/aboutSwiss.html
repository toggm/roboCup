About Swiss-system tournament

Swiss system is one of one of the most popular tournament systems for logic games.
It has these goals and benefits:

  • The system takes care of all players, does not focus itself only on the winners. Each player will have an estimate of his skill in the tournament. And even if your first opponent will be the future winner -- you'll still have a good chance to take a good place in the results table.
  • All players play an equal number of rounds. There are no idle rounds when you're only waiting for someone else to play.
  • With advancement of the tournament your opponents will be picked up closer to you by strength.

Formal rules

  • All players play an equal number of rounds.
  • Number of rounds depends on number of participants:
    5-8 players 9-16 players 17-32 players 33-64 players
    5 rounds 6 rounds 7 rounds 8 rounds
  • The goal of a player is to take as much tournament points as possible. Each win gives 4 points, each draw gives 2 points, each loss gives 1 point.
  • At start of each round all players are sorted by obtained points. The strongest player is matched in a game with the second. The third with the fourth. And so on. Minor permutations are used to avoid game duplicates (same opponents twice).
    Example. Suppose players had 17, 15, 14, 13, 13, 11 points. Then assigned games will be:
    "17"-"15",     "14"-"some of the 13",     "the other 13"-"11".
